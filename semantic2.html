header element

Introduction for the whole page or individual sections,article,nav,aside elements.
Typically contains site name, logo, navigation. Does not have to be at the beginning of page.

Example:


<header>
<h1> The importance of being earnest</h1>
<h3 > A quest for truth and Beauty</h3 >
<p > The play was written in 1895 by playwright Oscar wilde</p >
</header >

Footer element

Includes typical footer information like authoring, copyrights, contact information and a footer menu.

<footer>
<p >Written by OScar Wilde</p >
<p >Contact information: <a href=mailto:ocar@wilde.com>oscar@wilde.com</a ></p >
</footer >

nav element:

Navigation links for the document. A page can have more than one <nav >element like
table of contents, horizantal navigation in header and footer navigation.

<nav ><ol >
<li ><a href=/act1/>Act 1</a ><li >
<li ><a href=/act2/>Act 2</a ><li >
<li ><a href=/act3/>Act 3</a ><li >
</ol ><nav>

Section element

Defines sections in the document such as chapters, headers,etc. Typically used on
content that cannot make sense on its own


<Section >
<h1 >Act 1 - Scene 1</h1 >
<p>Set in the morning of Algy's flat in Half Moon Street</p>
</Section >

Article element

Defines independent content that should make sense on own outside of the
document such as newspaper articles, blog posts, etc.


<article >
<h1 >A blogger's analysis of this brilliant satire</h1 >
<p >This witty, sometimes conscious paly is Wilde's playground to
raise his progressive sentiments..</p > </article >

Aside element


Side content other than main content, like a sidebar. These are not considered
part of the main page outline.

<p > Algernon's flat is luxuriously and artistically furnished</p >
<aside >
<h3 >Algernon Moncrieff</h3 >
<p >A wealthy bachelor who lives in a fashionable part of london.
He has a good sense of humor and utter lack of respect for
society.</p >
</aside >

Details Elements

A way to provide additional information that the user can show or hide.
content that is shown to user by default. Other content is hidden and be expanded to view.


<details >
<summary >Cast Members</summary >
<p >George Washington as Algernon Moncrieff</p >
<p >Ronald Reagan as John Worthing</p >
</details >
